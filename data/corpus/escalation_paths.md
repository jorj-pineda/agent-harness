# Escalation Paths

## When to escalate

Escalate any ticket where the customer explicitly requests a supervisor, where the dollar value in dispute exceeds $500, where a safety concern is reported, where the same issue has been reopened three times, or where the customer's tone indicates imminent account cancellation. Uncertainty about policy is not a valid reason to escalate — check the corpus first and ask a peer before routing up.

## Tier 1

Tier 1 handles routine inquiries: order status, tracking lookups, return initiation, password reset triggering, and general product questions. Tier 1 agents can issue refunds up to $100 without approval. Everything beyond their approval ceiling is escalated to tier 2.

## Tier 2

Tier 2 handles warranty claims, refund amounts between $100 and $500, account-level changes after identity verification, and re-opened tickets. Tier 2 agents can issue refunds up to $500 and can make exceptions to the returns policy for premium and enterprise customers. Refunds beyond $500, contract disputes, and anything legally ambiguous goes to tier 3.

## Tier 3

Tier 3 is a small cross-functional team covering legal, finance, and engineering hand-offs. They handle refunds above $500, compliance requests (GDPR deletion, CCPA disclosure), recurring defect batches that require engineering investigation, and any contact from a lawyer or regulator. Tier 3 responds within 1 business day but may take longer to resolve depending on the nature of the escalation.
