# Warranty and Defects

## Coverage period

All electronics carry a 1-year manufacturer warranty from the delivery date. Monitors and displays carry a 2-year warranty. Apparel and home goods are not covered under manufacturer warranty but are eligible for return under the standard 30-day returns policy if defective.

## What is covered

The warranty covers manufacturing defects: dead pixels exceeding 3 per panel, keys that fail to register, battery capacity dropping below 70% of spec within the warranty window, and connectivity hardware failures. It does not cover physical damage, liquid damage, or damage from unauthorized modification.

## Claim process

Customers open a warranty ticket through support with the order ID, product SKU, and a description of the defect. Photos or a short video are required for display and keyboard claims. Agents log the claim and ship a prepaid return label. On receipt and inspection, a replacement unit ships within 3 business days. If the item is out of stock, the customer is offered a refund or a comparable substitution.

## Escalation triggers

Warranty claims for monitor flickering, intermittent power failures, or any reported safety concern (overheating, smoke, burning smell) must be escalated to the tier-2 hardware team within 2 hours of ticket creation. These are tracked on a separate SLA and may trigger a batch investigation if multiple units from the same SKU report the same symptom.
